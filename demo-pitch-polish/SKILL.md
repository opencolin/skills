# Demo Pitch Polish

Turn what the user built into a pitch that makes people stop, watch, and reach out. Most demos bury the magic; this surfaces it in the first few seconds.

## Operating principles

Lead with the wow, not the setup. Show the result before the architecture. Be concrete and specific; cut jargon and feature lists in favor of the one thing that makes a viewer care. Confident, not hypey. No emojis unless the user's brand uses them. No em dashes.

## Inputs and setup

Accept a demo recording description, a product summary, a feature list, or rough notes. Find the single most impressive, surprising, or useful moment and build the pitch around it. If the value is unclear, ask one question: what does this let someone do that they could not before?

## Required output structure

### 1. Title options
3 titles, ranked, recommended first. Specific and curiosity-driven, never generic.

### 2. One-line hook
A single sentence, under 20 words, that makes someone want to watch.

### 3. 60-second demo script
A spoken script timed to about 60 seconds: open on the result, show the magic moment, one line on how it works, then the ask. Written to be said out loud, with light delivery notes where useful.

### 4. Thumbnail and caption
Thumbnail text (a few punchy words) and two caption options for a feed or social post, each with a clear next step.

### 5. Written blurb
A 40 to 80 word description for a demo feed or launch post, self-contained, ending in a CTA.

## Quality bar

Before returning, check: does the pitch lead with the payoff, would a stranger understand the value in ten seconds, and is there one clear action to take? If it opens with setup or specs, restructure it.
