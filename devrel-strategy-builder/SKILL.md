# DevRel Strategy Builder

Build a developer relations strategy from the developer's point of view inward, and package it as something a founder or hiring manager will actually read. The strength is being specific to the product and honest about where adoption breaks, not a generic DevRel checklist.

## Operating principles

Start from the developer, not the company: what they are trying to do, where they get stuck, and what would make them adopt and advocate. Tie every play to a stage of the adoption funnel and a metric. Be concrete and opinionated; a strategy that could apply to any product is worthless. No emojis. No em dashes.

## Inputs and setup

Accept a product name and whatever context exists (docs, site, positioning, current DevRel motion). Identify the developer ICP and the single biggest friction in time-to-first-success before proposing anything. If framing is a job pitch, position the user as the operator who closes that gap.

## Required output structure

### 1. Developer ICP
Who the target developer is, what they build, and what they need. Specific personas beat a generic developer.

### 2. Adoption funnel and the leak
Awareness to first success to habit to advocacy, with the single biggest leak named and evidence for it. This is the spine of the strategy.

### 3. The plays
Content, community, product-education, and ecosystem plays mapped to funnel stages. For each: what it is, which leak it fixes, and the effort/impact read.

### 4. Channel strategy
Where the target developers actually are and how to reach them, ranked. Avoid spray-and-pray.

### 5. 90-day plan
A sequenced plan with the first 30 days concrete and the next 60 directional. First wins first.

### 6. Metrics
The few numbers that prove it working (time-to-first-success, activation, content-to-signup, community growth, advocacy), with a baseline-and-target frame.

### 7. Leave-behind
A tight, paste-ready one-pager summarizing the above for a founder or hiring manager.

## Quality bar

Before returning, check: is every play tied to a specific leak and metric, is the strategy unmistakably about this product, and could a founder act on the 90-day plan tomorrow? If it reads generic, sharpen it to the product.
