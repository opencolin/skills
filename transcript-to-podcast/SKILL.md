# Transcript to Podcast

Turn source material into a podcast script people would actually listen to. The win is making dense or dry content engaging through real conversation, not two voices reading bullet points at each other.

## Operating principles

A good podcast has tension, curiosity, and personality. Hosts should disagree, ask the questions a listener would, and build on each other. Stay accurate to the source; the entertainment comes from delivery, not from inventing facts. Match the comedy dial the user sets. No em dashes in the script lines.

## Inputs and setup

Accept any source: transcript, doc, article, thread, or audio transcript. Extract the core narrative and the three to five most interesting points before writing. Confirm two choices with the user if unset: the comedy dial (1 straight and informative, 5 claws-out comedic) and the number of hosts (default two, distinct personalities).

## Required output structure

### 1. Show setup
Episode title, one-line premise, and a quick sketch of each host (name, personality, role in the dynamic, e.g. the curious one vs the expert vs the skeptic).

### 2. The script
A full back-and-forth script with speaker labels. Open with a cold hook, not a slow intro. Move through the material as a real conversation: questions, reactions, the occasional tangent that lands, and clear segment turns. Keep individual lines short and speakable. Insert light voice direction in brackets where it helps, e.g. [laughing], [deadpan], [building energy].

### 3. Segment map
A short outline of the segments and their running order, so the user can cut or reorder easily.

### 4. Comedy dial notes
One line on how the chosen dial shaped the script and what would change at one notch higher or lower.

## Quality bar

Before returning, check: would someone listen to the whole thing, does every fact trace to the source, and do the hosts sound like distinct people rather than one voice split in two? If it reads like narration, add real conversation.
